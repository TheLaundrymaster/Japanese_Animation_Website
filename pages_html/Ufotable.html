Brief History:

Ufotable, Inc. (Japanese: ユーフォーテーブル有限会社, Hepburn: Yūfōtēburu yūgen-gaisha) is a Japanese animation studio founded in October 2000 by former TMS Entertainment staff through its subsidiary Telecom Animation Film and located in Nakano, Tokyo. A unique hallmark seen in many of their works (Ninja Nonsense, Futakoi Alternative, Coyote Ragtime Show, Gakuen Utopia Manabi Straight!, Tales of Symphonia , The Garden of Sinners) is a claymation sequence. Other notable works from the studio include Fate/Zero, Fate/stay night: Unlimited Blade Works and Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba.

Ryne's Take:

Ufotable is the most modern studio on this site and one of my personal favorites. I only just recently began to tune into the works of Ufotable after being pressured by a friend to watch Fate/Zero which is the beginning of the Fate series which is a more convoluted universe than the Marvel universe. I quickly became hooked by the style of animation of how nice all the animation looked and how you know watching it, by the animation, just how immensely powerful some characters are.

Just last year they released Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba AKA Demon Slayer. Which now is one of Ufotable's biggest franchises aside from the Fate series and shows the magic that they use to make amazing shows. The movie which recently came out in Japan in October 2020 in Japan is one of Japan's highest-grossing films, one of the highest-grossing anime films ever, and the world's eighth highest-grossing film of 2020. This shows just how much people love the animation that Ufotable is able to adapt to the screen and want more.
