Deep Dive into Japanese Animation Studios









Definition of anime:
Merriam-Webster: a style of animation originating in Japan that is characterized by stark colorful graphics depicting vibrant characters in action-filled plots often with fantastic or futuristic themes.

The Urban Dictionary: Japanese animation drawn in the same style as manga just animated. Often messed up by America.

source: https://www.urbandictionary.com/define.php?term=Anime
source: https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/anime?src=search-dict-box

Japanese animation studios are similar to US animation studios. Each studio specializes in a specific kind of content. For example, Universal Animation with South Park their focus is to make adult content while Nickelodeon and Cartoon Network make child friendly content. Japan animation studios works similar but not in the sense of the content but the animation styles and manga or stories they choose to animate. As you will see Kyoto Animation makes a majority drama or slice of life (a “slice” of the main character’s life) shows while Studio Trigger goes all in on action with over the top everything. It does not always mean that one studio can only make one genre of work, but they usually make that kind of work. I just discuss just seven studios here but there are hundreds more, each with their own audience and their own style of animating.

    Popular anime genres:
